Quanta Coffee Process

Quanta Coffee adds value throughout each individual process of producing coffee. The local Colombian farmers take pride in every layer of production to ensure the best quality of the unique and specialty coffees that we offer.

Planting Coffee

Each farm is different but the beginnings are the same, each coffee bean is germinated in a controlled area where with extra care it grows enough to be transplanted in a small plastic bag where, its allowed to grow bigger and stronger finally reaching the farm lands to grow another year and half until its first harvest.

Harvesting the Cherries

Each farmer has his/her way of harvesting their coffee, and they all agree that selectively hand-picked cherries is the best method to ensure a superb cup of coffee.

Processing the Cherries

All the farms we own and work with have their own wet mill to process the coffee cherries, we encourage and teach farmers to use additional methods of processing that in return produce a better cup of coffee and a more profitable business for each farmer.

Drying the Beans

-Each farm is different some place the processed coffee cherries on cement patios while others used whats commonly known as african beds which are raised bamboo and plastic mesh beds with its own plastic roof to protect the beans from getting wet.

Selecting the Beans

After our beans’ parchment skin is hulled off at our dry mill they fall into a series of perforated screens that do a pre-selection of size, after this has taken place we continue to hand select batches of coffee beans to assure quality.

Roasting the Coffee

Our coffees are roasted diligently by our master roaster, who roasts batches of 25 pounds using our IR-12 Diedrich Roaster.

Packaging the Coffee

Once each batch has cooled we package the coffee in 12 ounce bags, designed to keep our products fresh. These finished products are shipped by plane to our warehouse in Miami, Florida for distribution.
